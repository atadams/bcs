What are the benefits and harms of breast cancer screening for average-risk women aged 40 to 49 years?

These numbers are estimates from modeling. Modeling involves running data from the best available information in equations. Models do not predict the benefits and harms for an individual woman. Rather, they provide information for populations of women about various screening strategies (e.g., starting screening at age 40 vs 50). In addition, modeling makes assumptions about the population. For example, these models assumed that all women were average-risk and attended all screenings and received treatment.
